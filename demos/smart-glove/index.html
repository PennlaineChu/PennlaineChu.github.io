Vision

A smart glove that helps people with presbyopia read small text by providing instant magnification on a wrist-mounted OLED—controlled with natural gestures, no phone required.

  • Pinch to capture & magnify
  • Digital zoom 2× → 4× → 8×
  • Gesture-only UX
  • Fully wearable

Circuit diagram

Wire map

Power
LiPo red/black → TP4056 → board 5V/VIN + GND → on-board 3.3V to OLED, MPU6050, camera. JST-PH on the pack for unplug; JST-SH is for flex harnesses, not the battery.
Camera (CSI)
FPC ribbon + many parallel lines + clock/sync; plus I2C (SDA/SCL) for sensor setup. This is the “thick” link—usually not all dupont wires.
OLED (SPI)
Bench: GPIO18 SCK, GPIO11 MOSI (many boards label this pad SDA), GPIO17 CS, GPIO16 DC, GPIO15 RES/RST (+ 3.3V, GND, backlight). Short jumpers or PCB; confirm vs camera pinout.
MPU6050 (I2C)
SDA → GPIO8, SCL → GPIO9, + 3.3V + GND (MPU-6050). Same bus can share with the camera SCCB if addresses and pull-ups are OK. On ESP32-S3, do not use GPIO26–27 for I2C (flash/PSRAM). Use ~4.7 kΩ pull-ups to 3.3V on SDA/SCL if the module does not. Confirm GPIO8/9 are free on your camera/dev-kit pinout.
Flex sensors (ADC)
Five sensors, each a voltage divider → one ADC1 input per channel. Bench: GPIO1, 2, 4, 5, 6. Keep off GPIO8/9 (I²C) and GPIO11, 15–18 (OLED SPI). Recheck USB, camera, strapping (GPIO0, GPIO3). Finger harness: JST SH 1.0mm, not the battery connector. 
LiPo → TP4056 → ESP32 5V/VIN → 3.3V/GND → modules
SPI→OLED (18 SCK · 11 MOSI · 17 CS · 16 DC · 15 RES)  I2C*→MPU+cam  FPC→OV2640  ADC1→flex×5
*S3: I²C 8/9. ADC1 flex 1·2·4·5·6 — verify vs board.
